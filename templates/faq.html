Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Common symptoms include chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeat, and swelling in the legs, ankles, or feet. If you experience these symptoms, seek medical advice promptly.

Major risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, physical inactivity, and a family history of heart disease.

Diagnosis often involves a physical examination, ECG, stress tests, echocardiograms, and blood tests. Advanced imaging, such as CT or MRI scans, may also be used.

Yes, heart disease can often be prevented by maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, avoiding tobacco use, and managing stress levels.

Reducing cholesterol involves eating a heart-healthy diet, exercising regularly, avoiding saturated and trans fats, and possibly taking prescribed medications like statins.
